Isotope

What isotopes are and how they differ from each other.

What is an Isotope?

Isotopes are atoms of the same element that have the same number of protons (same atomic number Z) but a different number of neutrons (different mass number A). The term comes from the Greek words isos ("same") and topos ("place"), indicating that they occupy the same position in the periodic table.

Chemical vs. Physical Properties

Because chemical reactivity is determined by an atom's electron shell configuration (which is governed by the number of protons), isotopes of an element exhibit nearly identical chemical properties. For example, deuterium (heavy hydrogen) reacts with oxygen to form water (D2O, "heavy water") in the exact same stoichiometry as normal hydrogen.

However, because they differ in mass, isotopes exhibit different physical properties (such as density, melting point, boiling point, and diffusion rates) and wildly different nuclear properties (such as stability and radioactivity).

Average Atomic Mass Calculation

The atomic weight listed for elements on the periodic table is not the mass of a single atom. Instead, it is the weighted average of the masses of all naturally occurring isotopes of that element, based on their fractional abundance on Earth.

The formula for calculating relative atomic mass (Ar) is:

Ar = Σ (Isotope Mass × Fractional Abundance)

Detailed Examples

Example 1: The Isotopes of Hydrogen

Hydrogen has three naturally occurring isotopes, which are unique in having their own specific names:

  • Protium (11H): 1 proton, 0 neutrons. It constitutes 99.98% of natural hydrogen.
  • Deuterium (21H or D): 1 proton, 1 neutron. Stable, constitutes 0.02% of natural hydrogen. Used in nuclear reactors.
  • Tritium (31H or T): 1 proton, 2 neutrons. Radioactive (half-life of 12.3 years), trace amounts in nature. Used in fusion research.
Example 2: Chlorine Atomic Weight Math

Chlorine exists primarily as two stable isotopes: Chlorine-35 (mass ≈ 35 amu) with 75.77% abundance, and Chlorine-37 (mass ≈ 37 amu) with 24.23% abundance.

Weighted Average Calculation:

Ar(Cl) = (35 × 0.7577) + (37 × 0.2423) = 26.52 + 8.96 = 35.48 amu

This is why Chlorine is represented in the periodic table with an atomic mass of approximately 35.5.
