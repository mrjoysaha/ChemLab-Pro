Shells, Subshells, and Orbitals

In modern atomic physics, electrons are not treated as particles orbiting in fixed circles (as in the old Bohr model). Instead, they are described by quantum wave functions, which outline regions of probability. These states are structured hierarchically:

  • Shells (Energy Levels): The primary energy bands of the atom, denoted by the Principal Quantum Number (n). As n increases, the shell is further from the nucleus, and the electrons possess higher energy.
  • Subshells (Sublevels): Sub-divisions of each shell, representing different shapes of electron clouds. Subshells are denoted by letters: s, p, d, and f.
  • Orbitals: The specific 3D regions of space within a subshell where there is a 90% or higher probability of finding an electron. Each orbital can accommodate a maximum of 2 electrons.

The Four Quantum Numbers

Every electron in an atom is uniquely identified by a set of four quantum numbers:

  1. Principal Quantum Number (n): Defines the shell. Takes integer values: n = 1, 2, 3...
  2. Azimuthal/Angular Momentum Quantum Number (l): Defines the shape of the subshell. Takes values from 0 to n-1.
    • l = 0: s (Spherical)
    • l = 1: p (Dumbbell)
    • l = 2: d (Cloverleaf)
    • l = 3: f (Complex)
  3. Magnetic Quantum Number (ml): Defines the orientation of the orbital in space. Takes values from -l to +l, including zero. The total number of values represents the number of orbitals in that subshell.
  4. Spin Quantum Number (ms): Defines the spin orientation of the electron. Can only be +1/2 (spin-up ↑) or -1/2 (spin-down ↓).

Geometrical Shapes of Orbitals

The probability distributions (orbital shapes) are determined by mathematical solutions to the Schrödinger wave equation:

  • s-Orbitals (l = 0): Spherical and symmetrical around the nucleus. There is only one orientation (ml = 0).
  • p-Orbitals (l = 1): Dumbbell-shaped, with two lobes separated by a nodal plane at the nucleus. There are three orientations (ml = -1, 0, +1) aligned along the Cartesian axes: px, py, and pz.
  • d-Orbitals (l = 2): Cloverleaf-shaped with four lobes, except for the unique dz2 orbital which is a dumbbell surrounded by a donut-shaped ring. There are five orientations (ml = -2, -1, 0, +1, +2): dxy, dyz, dxz, dx2-y2, and dz2.

Detailed Examples

Example 1: Orbitals in the M-Shell (n = 3)

For the shell n = 3:

  • Allowed l values: 0 (s), 1 (p), 2 (d).
  • For l = 0 (3s): ml = 0 (1 orbital, capacity: 2 electrons)
  • For l = 1 (3p): ml = -1, 0, +1 (3 orbitals, capacity: 6 electrons)
  • For l = 2 (3d): ml = -2, -1, 0, +1, +2 (5 orbitals, capacity: 10 electrons)
  • Total Orbitals = 1 + 3 + 5 = 9 (which is n2 = 32). Total Electron Capacity = 2 × 9 = 18 electrons (which is 2n2).
