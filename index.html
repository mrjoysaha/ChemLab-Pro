What this covers

  • Reactive metal + acid → salt + hydrogen (using the standard reactivity series)
  • Reactive metal + water → metal hydroxide + hydrogen
  • Metal oxide + acid, acidic oxide + base, or metal oxide + acidic oxide → salt (+ water)
  • Acidic/basic oxide + water → acid or hydroxide
  • Acid + base → salt + water (neutralization)
  • Acid + carbonate/bicarbonate → salt + water + carbon dioxide
  • Metal + salt → single displacement, when the reactant metal is more reactive
  • Salt/carbonate/base + salt/carbonate/base → double displacement, when a precipitate forms (simplified solubility rules)
  • Metal + non-metal → direct combination into an ionic compound
  • Hydrocarbons and alcohols + O₂ → combustion (CO₂ + water), for any C/H/O fuel formula
  • A handful of named special cases: H₂ + O₂ → water, ammonia's weak-base equilibrium with water, the Haber process, and a few classic combinations

It doesn't attempt full organic chemistry, redox half-equations, or every possible oxidation state — variable-valency metals (Fe, Cu, Sn, Pb, Cr, Mn, Co, Ni, Hg, Au, Ti) are covered with their two most common charges. Solubility rules are the simplified version taught at introductory level, not a complete Ksp treatment.
